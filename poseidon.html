POSEIDON

Greek Name Transliteration Latin Spelling Roman Name
Ποσειδων Poseidôn Poseidon Neptunus, Neptune

Poseidon was the Olympian god of the sea, earthquakes, floods, drought and horses. He was usual always depicted holding a trident.

At birth, Poseidon was swallowed whole by his father Cronus, but Zeus later sought the help of the goddess Metis who fed the Titan an elixir causing him to spit out the god. During the War of the Titanes, the Cyclopes crafted a magical trident for Poseidon, and together with his brothers and sister he defeated the elder gods and imprisoned them in Tartarus.
