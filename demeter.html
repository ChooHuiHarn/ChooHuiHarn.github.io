DEMETER

Greek Name Transliteration Latin Spelling Roman Name
Δημητηρ Dêmêtêr Demeter Ceres

Demeter was the Greek goddess of agriculture, grain, and bread, sustaining mankind with the earth's rich bounty. She was often depicted wearing a crown, bearing sheafs of wheat, a cornucopia, or a torch. She was sometimes with animals like pigs, snakes, or cranes.

Demeter oversaw one of the leading Mystery Cults that offered its initiates a path to a blessed afterlife in Elysium. One of her most famous myths is about how her daughter, Persephone, was kidnapped, and she refused to let anything grow until her daughter returned.
